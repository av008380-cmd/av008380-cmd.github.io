Resilience is not endurance; it is recovery. We fluctuate between zones.

RED ZONE (Survival)
  • Tunnel vision
  • Defensiveness
  • "Amygdala Hijack"
  • Judgmental
GREEN ZONE (Learning)
  • Open to feedback
  • Creative
  • Empathetic
  • Optimistic

3. The Iceberg Model

To change behavior (visible), you must address what is below the waterline.

  • Above Water: Behaviors, Actions, Words.
  • The Waterline
  • Below Water: Thoughts ("I'm an imposter")
  • Below Water: Feelings (Fear, Anxiety)
  • Below Water: Values (Security, Achievement)

Structured Problem Solving

Step 1: Define (SMART)

A problem well defined is half solved.

  • Specific (What exactly?)
  • Measurable (How much?)
  • Actionable (Can we fix it?)
  • Relevant (Does it matter?)
  • Time-bound (By when?)

Step 2: Structure (MECE)

Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive.

No Overlaps
No Gaps

Step 3: Disaggregation (Logic Trees)

Issue Tree (The "How")

Use when you know the problem but not the solution.

Profit Down?
├── Revenue Down?
│   ├── Price too low?
│   └── Volume too low?
└── Costs Up?
    ├── Fixed costs?
    └── Variable costs?

Hypothesis Tree (The "Why")

Use when you have a hunch to test.

Hypothesis: Costs are up.
├── Material costs rose? (Test)
└── Labor costs rose? (Test)

Step 4: Prioritization (80/20 Rule)

Do not analyze the whole tree. Pick the high-impact branches.

High Impact / Low Effort (Quick Wins)
High Impact / High Effort (Major Projects)
Low Impact / High Effort (Avoid)

Introduction Framework: SCR

Use this to set the stage before revealing your Pyramid.

Situation: Uncontroversial facts everyone agrees on.
"We have grown 10% this year."
Complication: The change or problem that triggers action.
"However, new regulations will cut our margin."
Resolution: Your proposal (Link to Pyramid).
"Therefore, we must automate operations."

Relationships & Feedback

The Trust Equation

(C + R + I) / S
Credibility (Words) "I know my stuff"
Reliability (Actions) "I do what I say"
Intimacy (Emotions) "I feel safe with you"
Self-Orientation (Motives) "I care about myself"
High Self-Orientation divides (lowers) the total trust score.

The SBI Feedback Model

Feedback must be specific and non-judgmental.

Situation
"In the client meeting yesterday morning..." (Anchor in time/place)
Behavior
"...you interrupted the stakeholder twice..." (Observable fact, not "you were rude")
Impact
"...it caused them to become quiet and we missed their requirements." (The result)

My Digital Toolkit

Design Thinking

A user-centric approach to innovation.

  1. Empathize: Understand the user's pain.
  2. Define: State the user's problem.
  3. Ideate: Brainstorm without limits.
  4. Prototype: Build a rough version.
  5. Test: Get feedback and iterate.

Agile Ways of Working

Iterative delivery over perfect planning.

  • Sprints: Short 2-week work cycles.
  • MVP: Minimum Viable Product.
  • Retrospectives: "What went well? What didn't?"
  • Stand-ups: Daily 15-min syncs.
